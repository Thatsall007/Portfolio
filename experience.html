Quant Analyst Intern
Walford Capitals Pvt. Ltd.          (Jan 2025 - Present)

As part of my project in financial data analysis, I am utilizing Python to analyze historical stock data. The project involves multiple steps to clean, process, and visualize the data, along with creating a simple trading strategy based on moving averages. I am starting by using the `yfinance` library to download historical stock data, focusing on the daily closing prices. After retrieving the data, I am performing data cleaning by handling missing values and removing duplicates to ensure the dataset is accurate and ready for analysis.

Next, I am visualizing the trends in the stock's closing prices using Matplotlib. This is helping me identify the overall movement of the stock over time, providing valuable insights for further analysis. For technical analysis, I am computing moving averages using Pandas. Moving averages are crucial in identifying trends in stock price movements, so I am applying both short-term and long-term moving averages to the dataset. Building on this, I am implementing a simple trading strategy using moving average crossover logic. This involves generating "Buy" and "Sell" signals based on the point where the short-term moving average crosses above or below the long-term moving average. I am adding these signals as new columns in the dataset to provide actionable insights.

Finally, I am visualizing the stock price chart with the generated Buy and Sell signals overlaid. This visual representation is making it easy to track potential trading opportunities and analyze how the moving averages are performing in real-time market conditions. Through this process, I am gaining valuable experience in data manipulation, technical analysis, and financial data visualization.
